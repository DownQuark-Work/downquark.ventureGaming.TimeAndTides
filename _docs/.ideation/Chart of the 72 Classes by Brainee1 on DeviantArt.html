Chart of the 72 Classes

Published: Feb 1, 2011
By
Brainee1
10K Views

Description

Journeyman: This is where everyone starts out. Basically, if you start adventuring, fighting for profit, or participate in any number of other "Journeymanish" activities, you automatically become a Journeyman. At this point, you are required to register with the Guild so they can keep close tabs on you and make you their zombie slave if need be. Great fun to be had by all. The basic privileges and restrictions of Journeymen are: Free and cheap training in combat and stuff, and the Guild assures you that all the dungeons you enter are safe and at your skill level; unfortunately there are no bathrooms in these dungeons. Or, usually, bushes to go behind.

Page: By advancing to any tier 1 class, you take on the rights and responsibilities of the sub-guild, while retaining the ones from the Journeyman tier. You are also apprenticed under a skilled practitioner of... well, killing. In the Page's case, that would be a melee warrior. Pages have, likely, the hardest time of things. Unlike the other tier 1 classes, they fight in the mud and blood and oozes of all shapes (Don't ask) and colors. Nothing interesting here. Just... lots of gore. Oh, and swords. But not SHINY swords. Pages need to WORK to get that clean slice through a goblin skull they saw on TV. And their food is always tainted with rust!

Rookie: Rookies train under archers. Generally speaking, an archer is not that useful against someone in full plate armor, but remember, this is fiction. These archers are magical, and have giant bows that are strung with the guts of dragons... or boars. Boars work too. So does slime-ooze soaked cloth, in a pinch. Archers are archers. Nothing to say here, just keep moving. Unless you like archers. Then you get to wait. Because I don't feel like making a humorous outline for the rest of the archer classes right now. (By the way, whenever I mention "class", understand that it's synonymous with "guild".)

Tinker: I'm sure you're wondering what this is doing here. This is a remnant of the old days, back when people depended on technology rather than magic to run their lives. Sound familiar? Well I made it up myself so everyone else just copied my idea before I had it >.<. Basically the world is sort of "post-apocalyptic", with magic and technology growing up as the two bases of learning and the scientific method on Earth. Oh, and Tinkers build things. No, not like Bob... like this guy: [link]

Apprentice: And these are the guys who destroyed the world and made everyone start over from scratch. Well... not these guys SPECIFICALLY... Apprentices apprentice under mages and the like. They use magic. For those taking notes, magic comes in two flavors: "Mana", or "Soul", and "Raivo", or "This word doesn't exist, don't even try looking it up.". Mana-based spells use your soul as a form of energy to basically obliterate anything in your way. If your foes weren't Carbon-based, they'd vaporize. As it stands, they liquefy. Raivo is "junk" anger, tossed back from the Void Realm (more on this later). It's less powerful, but there's a lot more of it. And you aren't tearing a chunk of your own soul out.

Recruit: I'll bet you're wondering why I saved the boring one for last, aren't you? That's not because it's boring, but because it's stupidly complex. Mercenaries get jobs, get paid for doing these jobs, then get other jobs. As a note, they also lose Journeyman status upon becoming a Recruit, and are not trained before being put into the work force. Here's how it works: Do lots of jobs, get rich, buy your way into higher stations. Die, and some random adventurer comes along and takes all your loot.

Mercenary: From here it's pretty simple. Do lots job, become Mercenary. Get moar job.

Elite Mercenary: I'm not even going to say anything about this one.

Leader: This position is wanted by many, owned by one. The richest (wo)man on Earth is whoever leads the mercenaries guild at any given time.

Fighter: Fighters fight. With swords. And stuff. Fighter is a tier two class, and all tier two classes are free of the rules of the Journeyman's Guild (They're still in it though, just in a different part of it). Nothing much to say about tier 2 classes. Just what they become.

Soldier: Foot soldiers. They get paid to parade about in fancy armor and let citizens know that they're safe. A good job, although somewhat restricting.

Warrior: I know, the names are just amazing. Infantry adventurers. Use weapons.

Knight: You get your horse now. Knights use swords, lances, and of course, horse feet, as their weapons of choice. Not very good in dungeons, however...

Guard: Whoever hired you decided to give you a cushy desk job. Now you get to sit around all day and attack drunk people and beggars because you're so bored.

Swordsman: Every game has one. The dude that has a big sword and carries it around as if it's a twig. Or a katana. Or a sword and shield. Or a...
Actually, they use axes too. And spears. And whatever else they can get their hands on.

Paladin: Better horse. A few spells. And the requirement of being stuck-up and self-righteous.

Veteran: Better armor, better weapons.

Thief: Pickpockets. The lot of 'em. No one knows quite how they managed to get a guild okay'd by the council, but they did. And now they steal stuff using government money.

Arcane Bladesman: What happens when you give a mage a sword and heavy armor? This guy does! Although Arcane Bladesmen generally aren't as skilled in magic as a full-fledged mage, they can give many people a run-for-their-money when they decide swords and lasers just aren't cool enough when used separately.

Dragon Knight: Well, it's a knight... who rides... a dragon. Inconceivable, I know.

Javalineer: [link]
Moving on...

Juggernaut: People in huge suits of armor. Generally over a story tall, Juggernauts are strategically used to place a moving wall on the battlefield. Generally speaking Juggernauts are slow, but if they manage to hit, the foe will be down and out for a long time.

Wyrmbane*: Because Swordsman was lonely.

*Wyrmbane has actually been discontinued for some time, I don't know why it's on there. It was replaced by the Blade Dancer class.

Blade Dancer: Blade Dancers use a small, hilt-less, blade which can be held in many ways. Unlike most other swordsmen, Blade Dancers focus on training, not equipment. It is generally considered taboo by the Blade Dancers to use their blades for any other reason than to kill a foe, and taboo to kill a foe for any other reason than your own life being in danger. Blade Dancers are said to be able to deflect blades with their fists.

Elemental Stingerblade: Arcane Bladesman, but with a rapier, a cooler shield, and fireballs.

Assassin: No one's ever made it out of this guild alive. Or so they say.

Windchaser: Spear users who focus on training their cross-country skills. Said to be able to run on top of sound waves, there's no doubting they're capable of creating sonic booms when they run. They generally carry a spear with a laser gun enhancement.

Creative juices running dry, I'll finish later.
